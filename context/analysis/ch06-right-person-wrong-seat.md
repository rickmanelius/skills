# Chapter 6: Right Person, Wrong Seat (Or Just Wrong Person)

## Chapter Summary
Team evaluation using structured frameworks. Covers startup employee qualities, complementary skills mapping, team stage assessment, and the B-player decision.

## Candidate Skills

### `/team-quality-audit`
- **What it does**: Evaluates each team member against five dimensions: Exponential vs. Linear, Missionaries vs. Mercenaries, Product vs. Project mindset, Resourceful vs. Needing directions, Startup vs. BigCo experience.
- **Universality**: 8 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 8 | **Total**: 24
- **Key quote**: "I want people who can find their way off a desert island. I don't need to hand a map to. I want them to build the map."

### `/team-health-check`
- **What it does**: Assesses team stage (Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing) via Tuckman's model, scores against a high-performance checklist.
- **Universality**: 9 | **Power**: 7 | **AI-transferability**: 9 | **Total**: 25
- **Key framework**: High-Performance Team Checklist — autonomy, results over process, clear goals, async work, mission driven, rapid feedback, accountability, stretch goals, daily heartbeat, mutual respect.

### `/b-player-decision`
- **What it does**: Guides through the retain-or-release decision for B-level performers using a single forcing question.
- **Universality**: 8 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 7 | **Total**: 23
- **The forcing question**: "Would you give this person a job now, given everything you know about them?"
- **Key insight**: "B players are afraid of A players because they make them look weak."

### `/team-skills-gap`
- **What it does**: Maps team's complementary skills against the Three Machines, identifies coverage gaps, recommends next exponential hire.
- **Universality**: 7 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 8 | **Total**: 23
