# Chapter 5: Does Your Story Inspire and Sell?

## Chapter Summary
Pitch development through a progressive maturity model: verbal pitch (five-finger), structured deck (Sequoia template), and optimized presentation. Strong on the iterative refinement process.

## Candidate Skills

### `/five-finger-pitch`
- **What it does**: Guides you through crafting a five-minute, five-finger verbal pitch: company/product, money/ROI, team/feasibility, sales/scale, protection/defensibility.
- **Universality**: 9 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 9 | **Total**: 26
- **Key framework**: Thumb (product), Index (money), Middle (team), Ring (scale), Pinky (protection). Closing: "On a scale of 0-10, how motivated are you to take this further?"

### `/sequoia-deck-builder`
- **What it does**: Walks through building a pitch deck using the Sequoia 12-slide template with guided prompts and quality checks.
- **Universality**: 7 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 9 | **Total**: 24
- **Key framework**: 12 slides — Title, Purpose, Problem, Solution, Why Now, Market, Competition, Product, Business Model, Team, Financials, CTAs

### `/pitch-deck-audit`
- **What it does**: Evaluates an existing pitch deck against quality criteria: compelling, concise, self-consistent. Uses "So what?" / "No f---ing way" test.
- **Universality**: 8 | **Power**: 9 | **AI-transferability**: 8 | **Total**: 25
- **Key insight**: Andy Miller — changing sequence from present-qualify-close to qualify-close-present changed conversion from 2% to 40%.
