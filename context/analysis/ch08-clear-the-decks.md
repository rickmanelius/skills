# Chapter 8: Clear the Decks

## Chapter Summary
Creating space by auditing and reclaiming time. Progressive framework: schedule sacred time, audit current usage, ruthlessly eliminate, add safeguards, design ideal future state.

## Candidate Skills

### `/time-audit`
- **What it does**: Week-long time tracking exercise — log every 30 minutes, categorize, compare actual vs. ideal allocation.
- **Universality**: 9 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 8 | **Total**: 25
- **Key insight**: Most founders find 20-40% of their time is unplanned/reactive.
- **Categories**: Product, Dev, Marketing, Sales, Bizdev, Accounting, Support, Legal, Admin, Unplanned

### `/ruthless-elimination`
- **What it does**: Reclaim time using three levers — Deletion, Delegation, and Delay — applied to every low-value activity from the time audit.
- **Universality**: 9 | **Power**: 9 | **AI-transferability**: 9 | **Total**: 27
- **Key framework**: The 3 D's — Delete (drop immediately), Delegate (who else can do this?), Delay (revisit in 30/60/90 days)
- **Key estimate**: Most founders spend 5-15 hours/week on things that could be deleted, delegated, or delayed.

### `/ideal-week-design`
- **What it does**: Proactively design your ideal week — what to work on, when, and how days are structured.
- **Universality**: 8 | **Power**: 7 | **AI-transferability**: 8 | **Total**: 23
- **Key reframe**: From "Here's what I did" to "Here's what I'd like to be doing."

### `/time-safeguards`
- **What it does**: Establishes protective systems to prevent backsliding: Sunday night prep, calendar firewall, app blockers, identity change, weekly re-audit.
- **Universality**: 8 | **Power**: 7 | **AI-transferability**: 7 | **Total**: 22
