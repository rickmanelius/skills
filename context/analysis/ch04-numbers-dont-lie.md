# Chapter 4: The Numbers Don't Lie

## Chapter Summary
The financial reality check. Covers burn rate, runway, default alive, business model analysis, and structured assumption auditing. Contains the most quantitatively rigorous frameworks in the book.

## Candidate Skills

### `/burn-runway-check`
- **What it does**: Calculates burn rate, runway, and default alive threshold. Establishes monitoring cadence.
- **Universality**: 8 | **Power**: 9 | **AI-transferability**: 9 | **Total**: 26
- **Key quote**: "If you don't know these, you are flying blind."
- **Key metrics**: CAC, COGS, churn rate, year-over-year growth

### `/what-game`
- **What it does**: Maps the specific "game" your business model is playing — profitability stages, inflection points, no-man's-land zones, required scale.
- **Universality**: 8 | **Power**: 9 | **AI-transferability**: 8 | **Total**: 25
- **Key example**: Franchise game — 0-3 (not profitable), 4-8 (profitable), 8-20 (no-man's-land), 20+ (economies of scale)
- **Key quote**: "Any number times zero is still zero, so no amount of volume will fix the bad model."

### `/assumptions-audit`
- **What it does**: Walks through every cost and revenue assumption using structured checklists across 8 categories: Personnel, OPEX, CAPEX, COGS, Revenue Targets, Funnel, Product Tiers, Business Model, Growth.
- **Universality**: 9 | **Power**: 9 | **AI-transferability**: 10 | **Total**: 28
- **Key quote**: "90 percent of startup founders are either unwilling or unable to do this until someone forces them to do it."

### `/napkin-math`
- **What it does**: Quick back-of-napkin calculation: start with revenue target, work backward through conversion ratios to determine required growth. Exposes feasibility.
- **Universality**: 9 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 10 | **Total**: 27
- **Key approach**: "We start with the end in mind and walk backward."
- **Key quote**: "The goal here isn't accuracy; it's to check whether or not you have some sense of a reasonable way to get to the target."

### `/pro-forma-stress-test`
- **What it does**: Stress-tests assumptions across four scenarios: base case, conservative (3x worse), exceptional (5x better), and worst case (zero revenue).
- **Universality**: 8 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 8 | **Total**: 24

### `/delusion-check`
- **What it does**: Compares model's required numbers against reality to determine viability, forcing a pivot-or-persist decision based on evidence.
- **Universality**: 7 | **Power**: 9 | **AI-transferability**: 7 | **Total**: 23
