# Chapter 3: Product Gut Check — Would People Use Your Product If You Gave It Away For Free?

## Chapter Summary
Five structured "Product Checks" that assess product-market fit from the customer's perspective. Strong frameworks for positioning, voice-of-customer research, and adoption optimization.

## Candidate Skills

### `/wiifm-check`
- **What it does**: Forces you to articulate value through the customer's selfish lens ("What's in it for me?") using 7 core questions a prospect silently asks.
- **Universality**: 9 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 9 | **Total**: 26
- **Key framework**: 7 questions — Why should I care? What's in it for me? How will I use this? Save me time? Make me money? Give me status? Make my life better?
- **Key quote**: "If the customer can't mentally answer these questions pretty damn quick, you will be met with the worst rejection of all: apathy."

### `/voice-of-customer`
- **What it does**: Guides you to mine public forums, reviews, and interviews to capture the exact words customers use to describe their problem.
- **Universality**: 9 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 9 | **Total**: 26
- **Key framework**: Jay Abraham trick — find how customers talk about the problem in Amazon reviews, forums, social media. Copy phrases verbatim.
- **Key quote**: "It's like you're reading my diary."

### `/wants-needs-shoulds`
- **What it does**: Classifies whether your product solves a want (emotion), need (logic), or should (resistance), then assesses motivation alignment.
- **Universality**: 8 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 8 | **Total**: 24
- **Key insight**: "Your ideal product or service is both a need and a want." Shoulds are the hardest hill to climb.

### `/time-to-wow`
- **What it does**: Audits the path from first use to first positive experience, then systematically reduces friction, steps, and time.
- **Universality**: 9 | **Power**: 9 | **AI-transferability**: 8 | **Total**: 26
- **Key framework**: David Skok's "time to wow" — 9-question audit checklist. Success criteria: 80% get through steps, 80% experience aha moment, setup < 5 minutes.

### `/what-would-it-take`
- **What it does**: When facing a "no," ask "What would it take to say yes?" — shifting from adversarial rejection to collaborative problem-solving.
- **Universality**: 9 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 7 | **Total**: 24
- **Key quote**: "They are no longer an adversary sitting across the table, tight lipped, with arms folded."

### `/free-test`
- **What it does**: Forces the fundamental question: "Would people use your product if you gave it away for free?" If no, you have a product problem, not a price problem.
- **Universality**: 8 | **Power**: 9 | **AI-transferability**: 7 | **Total**: 24
- **Key lesson**: "We didn't have a price problem. We had a product problem."
