# Chapter 2: Captain's Gut Check — Delusional, Pretender, or the Real Deal?

## Chapter Summary
The richest early chapter for extractable skills. Contains four explicit "Gut Checks" that are structured self-assessment frameworks, plus a taxonomy of founder types. Highly structured and AI-transferable.

## Candidate Skills

### `/founder-type-check`
- **What it does**: Helps a founder honestly assess whether they are operating as Delusional, Pretender, or Real Deal — using behavioral patterns for each type.
- **Universality**: 9 | **Power**: 9 | **AI-transferability**: 8 | **Total**: 26
- **Key framework**: The Delusional (idea is paramount, market is wrong), The Pretender (enthusiastic but no follow-through, tackles easy tasks not critical ones), The Real Deal (brutal self-reflection is the price of admission)
- **Key quote**: "A visionary may have an insanely ambitious goal, but they know there is a price to be paid to reach it."

### `/accountability-test`
- **What it does**: 15-question accountability test to determine whether a founder is taking true ownership. Scoring reveals owner vs. passive participant.
- **Universality**: 9 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 9 | **Total**: 26
- **Key framework**: 15 questions (buck stops, hiring, firing, mission, products, budget, fundraising, direction, culture, market changes, competition, tough calls, legal, targets, mission influence). Correct answer: "Me" on every one.
- **Key quote**: "Lack of accountability is a lack of ownership."

### `/responsibility-test`
- **What it does**: 11-question responsibility/delegation test to assess whether work is appropriately distributed or bottlenecked through the founder.
- **Universality**: 8 | **Power**: 7 | **AI-transferability**: 9 | **Total**: 24
- **Key framework**: 11 questions. Correct answer: "All of us." If you answer "Me" to any, you may be bottlenecking the team.

### `/capability-willingness-matrix`
- **What it does**: 2x2 self-assessment on Capability vs. Willingness across four dimensions (Skills, Experience, Leadership, Doing Hard Things).
- **Universality**: 9 | **Power**: 9 | **AI-transferability**: 9 | **Total**: 27
- **Key insight**: "Lack of willingness is the bigger red flag/killer."
- **Follow-up questions**: gaps to address, timeline, inability vs. unwillingness, consequences, delegation, hiring needs

### `/three-machines-audit`
- **What it does**: Maps leadership and responsibilities across three "machines" (Company, Product, Customer) using a RACI matrix to identify gaps and single points of failure.
- **Universality**: 8 | **Power**: 9 | **AI-transferability**: 10 | **Total**: 27
- **Key framework**: Brad Feld's Three Machines + RACI overlay. One and only one person accountable per machine. No one person spans all three.

### `/wish-or-must`
- **What it does**: Forces a founder to confront whether their startup is a "wish" (plan B exists) or a "must" (existential commitment).
- **Universality**: 8 | **Power**: 8 | **AI-transferability**: 7 | **Total**: 23
- **Key quote**: "For me, it's always a must — a must or quit."
- **Timeline test**: "You're ready, willing, and able for the next three to ten years."
