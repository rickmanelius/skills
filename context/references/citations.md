# Citations & Attributions

Many frameworks in Save Your Startup build on or adapt work from other thinkers. This file ensures proper credit is given in every skill description.

## Frameworks Adapted From Other Creators

These skills are based on frameworks originated by others. Rick's adaptations are noted, but the original creator should be **prominently credited** in the skill description.

| Skill | Original Creator | Framework | Rick's Adaptation |
|-------|-----------------|-----------|-------------------|
| `/paint-done` | **Brene Brown** | "Paint Done" — collaborative alignment exercise | Applied to startup execution; structured as requester/implementer dialogue with iterative clarification |
| `/fear-setting` | **Tim Ferriss** | Fear-setting exercise (from *The 4-Hour Workweek* / TED talk) | Added 0-10 numerical rating on effort/pain/likelihood; notes fears rated 7-9 routinely drop to 2-5 |
| `/time-to-wow` | **David Skok** | "Time to Wow" concept | Built a 9-question audit checklist with success criteria (80/80 rule, <5 min setup) |
| `/break-bottleneck` | **Eliyahu Goldratt** | Theory of Constraints (from *The Goal*) | Applied to startup team throughput via a 5-step diagnostic loop |
| `/three-machines-audit` | **Brad Feld** | Three Machines (Company, Product, Customer) | Overlaid RACI matrix to identify gaps and single points of failure |
| `/team-health-check` | **Bruce Tuckman** | Stages of group development (Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing) | Layered a High-Performance Team Checklist on top of Tuckman's stages |
| `/sequoia-deck-builder` | **Sequoia Capital** | 12-slide pitch deck template | Added guided prompts and quality checks per slide as a walkthrough |
